Homebrew games

Homebrew games

The modern Genesis and Mega Drive homebrew catalog is large and genuinely impressive, with many releases rivaling or exceeding the production values of original retail titles. These are not just tech demos; they are complete, polished games sold on real cartridges and playable on flashcarts or emulators.

What you can find

The scene covers nearly every genre. There are tight platformers, shoot-em-ups that push the hardware's sprite limits, role-playing games with original stories, puzzle titles, and faithful ports of arcade classics. Some developers specialize in recreating the look and feel of the console's commercial heyday, while others deliberately break old conventions to show what the hardware can still do.

How games are made

Most contemporary releases are built with SGDK, a free toolkit that lets developers program in C with libraries for sprites, sound, and input. Others are written in 68000 assembly for maximum control. Music is often composed using trackers that target the console's YM2612 FM synthesis chip and PSG, giving authentic period-correct sound.

Supporting developers

Buying official cartridge releases directly funds the creators and the continued growth of the scene. Many titles are also released as free ROMs for those who want to try before they buy or who play primarily on flashcarts.

Getting started

If you are new, load a few well-regarded free ROMs onto a flashcart first. It is the fastest way to gauge how far the homebrew community has pushed this hardware.
